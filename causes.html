What are the causes of deforestation?

Deforestation can happen quickly, such as when a fire sweeps through the landscape or the forest is clear-cut to make way for an oil palm plantation. While deforestation appears to be declining in some countries, it remains disturbingly high in othersâ€”including Brazil and the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

  • Logging Activities

    Cutting down trees for wood, called logging, is another major cause. This wood is used for furniture, paper, and building materials. Even selective logging, where only certain trees are cut, can hurt forests by weakening their ecosystems.

  • Fires

    Forest fires, whether started by nature or people, can destroy trees, shrubs, and the top layer of soil. Pollution from nearby factories or farms can also harm trees and plants, making it harder for the forest to function properly.
