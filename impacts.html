Impacts

Deforestation has a wide range of significant and interconnected impacts across environmental, social.

 

Environmental Impacts:

  • Forests are like homes for most of the different kinds of plants and animals in the world. When people cut down forests, the animals and plants lose their homes. This also breaks the big forest into smaller pieces. Because they lose their homes, many kinds of animals and plants are in danger and could disappear forever. People think that we lose many different kinds of animals and plants every day because of cutting down forests.

Social Impacts:

  • It hurts the way they live.

  • When forests are gone, many people lose their way to make a living. Think about people who sell wood or things they find in the forest. They might not have jobs anymore if there are no trees. Forests also help us in other ways, like giving us clean water and good soil for growing food. If the forests disappear, it can be harder to get these things. So, when deforestation happens, people can become poorer and might not have enough food to eat.
